## Answers & Explanations

### LOD 1

**Q1 [LOD1 Q2]**
The first digit can only be 2 (1 way). The remaining three places are filled from the 7 remaining digits: 7 × 6 × 5 = 210.

---

**Q2 [LOD1 Q8]**
A straight line requires selecting any 2 of the 8 non-collinear points. ⁸C₂ = 28.

---

**Q3 [LOD1 Q11]**
Rearrangements exclude the original word. Total arrangements = 5!/2! = 60. Rearrangements = 60 − 1 = 59.

---

**Q4 [LOD1 Q13]**
Fix P at start and T at end: P _ _ _ T. The three remaining positions are filled with A, N, A → 3!/2! = 3 ways.

---

**Q5 [LOD1 Q21]**
Substituting r = 7: ²⁸C₁₄ / ²⁴C₁₀ simplifies to 225:11. Hence r = 7.

---

**Q6 [LOD1 Q22]**
ⁿCᵣ is maximised when r = n/2. With n = 20, invite r = 10 friends to each party.

---

**Q7 [LOD1 Q23]**
Identical items with a minimum-1 constraint uses the stars-and-bars formula ⁽ⁿ⁻¹⁾C₍ᵣ₋₁₎ with n = 10, r = 6 → ⁹C₅.

---

**Q8 [LOD1 Q27]**
ALLAHABAD has only 1 distinct vowel (A) and 4 distinct consonants (L, H, B, D). Ways = 1 × ⁴C₁ = 4.

---

**Q9 [LOD1 Q28]**
Choose 1 for the single room, 2 from the remaining 6 for the double room, and 4 from the remaining 4 for the large room: ⁷C₁ × ⁶C₂ × ⁴C₄ = 7!/(1!·2!·4!).

---

**Q10 [LOD1 Q29]**
From suit 1: 13 choices; suit 2: 12 (one value used); suit 3: 11; suit 4: 10. Total = 13 × 12 × 11 × 10.

---

**Q11 [LOD1 Q32]**
Total arrangements = 12!. Arrangements with best and worst together: treat them as one block → 2! × 11!. Answer = 12! − 2·11!.

---

**Q12 [LOD1 Q47]**
Arrange 7 digits where 3 are identical (digit 3) and 4 are identical (digit 5): 7!/(3!·4!) = 35.

---

**Q13 [LOD1 Q50]**
ⁿC₂ = 45 → n(n−1)/2 = 45 → n = 10.

---

**Q14 [LOD1 Q56]**
Arrange 5 boys in a circle: (5−1)! = 24 ways. Place 3 girls in 5 gaps between boys: ⁵P₃ = 60. Total = 24 × 60 = 1440.

---

**Q15 [LOD1 Q57]**
Case 1 — Quants book taken: DI can also be taken; choose 2 more from remaining 6 → ⁶C₂ = 15.
Case 2 — Quants not taken: DI also excluded; choose 3 from remaining 5 → ⁵C₃ = 10.
Total = 15 + 10 = 25.

---

**Q16 [LOD1 Q58]**
Both Radha & Mohan join: choose 3 more from 10 → ¹⁰C₃ = 120.
Neither joins: choose 5 from 10 → ¹⁰C₅ = 252.
Total = 120 + 252 = 372.

---

**Q17 [LOD1 Q62]**
Non-adjacent pairs = ⁿC₂ − N = 44 (since 88 min ÷ 2 min = 44 pairs). Solving N(N−1)/2 − N = 44 → N = 11.

---

**Q18 [LOD1 Q63]**
ᴮC₂ = 36 → B = 9. ᴳC₂ = 66 → G = 12. Total matches = ²¹C₂ = 210. Mixed matches = 210 − 36 − 66 = 108.

---

**Q19 [LOD1 Q67]**
Words starting with A: 8!/(2!·3!) = 3360; with B: 8!/(2!·4!) = 840; with D: 840; with H: 840.
Running total before L: 3360 + 840 + 840 + 840 = 5880.
Words starting with LAA: 6!/2! = 360; starting with LAB before LABA: 0 extra.
Words starting with LABAA: 4! = 24; starting with LABADAAHL, LABADAALH, LABADAHAL, LABADAHLA come before LABADALAH.
Total = 5880 + 360 + 24 + 4 + 1 = 6269.

---

**Q20 [LOD1 Q74]**
Place 3 white flags first — valid positions (no same-colour adjacent, different ends) are {1,3,5} and {2,4,6} only (2 ways). For each, arrange 1 red and 2 blue flags in the 3 remaining positions: 3!/2! = 3 ways. Total = 2 × 3 = 6.

---

### LOD 2

**Q21 [LOD2 Q2]**
Count by cases (digits include two 6s):
- Numbers ending in 0 with two 6s: ⁵C₁ × 3!/2! = 15
- Numbers ending in 5 with two 6s, starting with 6: ⁵C₁ × 2! = 10; not starting with 6: 4
- Numbers ending in 0 (one or no 6): ⁶C₃ × 3! = 120
- Numbers ending in 5 (one or no 6): ⁵C₁ × ⁵C₂ × 2! = 100
Total = 15 + 10 + 4 + 120 + 100 = 249.

---

**Q22 [LOD2 Q4]**
Fix G at the end. Arrange remaining 9 letters M, A, N, A, E, M, E, N, T with pairs M, A, E, N each appearing twice: 9!/(2!·2!·2!·2!) = 9!/(2!)⁴.

---

**Q23 [LOD2 Q6]**
A rectangle needs 2 horizontal lines from V and 2 vertical lines from W: ᵛC₂ × ʷC₂.

---

**Q24 [LOD2 Q10]**
3 fixed on one side, 2 fixed on the other. Choose 1 more for the side with 3 from remaining 7: ⁷C₁. Choose 2 more for the side with 2 from remaining 6: ⁶C₂. Arrange each side of 4: 4! × 4!. Total = 7 × 15 × 24 × 24 = 60,480.

---

**Q25 [LOD2 Q13]**
Treat n₂₈n₂₉ as a single fixed-order block. Arrange 99 objects (98 balls + 1 block) in 99! ways. Since the order within the block is fixed (n₂₈ must precede n₂₉), no further division needed: 99!.

---

**Q26 [LOD2 Q15]**
Seat R people on side 1 (ᴹPᴿ ways), S people on side 2 (ᴹPˢ ways), then place remaining 2M−R−S people in the leftover seats.

---

**Q27 [LOD2 Q26]**
Choose 3 positions for A, B, C from 8 — they need no internal arrangement (A < B < C is the only valid order). Arrange the other 5 in the remaining spots: ⁸C₃ × 5! = 8!/3!.

---

**Q28 [LOD2 Q27]**
Total ways to place 4 letters = 4!. Only 1 arrangement is fully correct. Wrong arrangements = 4! − 1 = 23.

---

**Q29 [LOD2 Q29]**
Choose 2 of the 4 letters to swap into each other's envelopes (the other 2 go correctly): ⁴C₂ = 6.

---

**Q30 [LOD2 Q38]**
Arrange 7 boys in a circle: (7−1)! = 6! ways. Place 7 girls in the 7 gaps between boys: 7! ways. Total = 6! × 7!.
